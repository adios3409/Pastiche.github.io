Rococo Poster

Rococo, style in interior design, the decorative arts, painting, architecture, and sculpture that originated in Paris in the early 18th century but was soon adopted throughout France and later in other countries, principally Germany and Austria. Rococo takes its name from the French word 'rocaille' , which means rock or broken shell – natural motifs that often formed part of the designs, along with fish and other marine decorations. Rococo started as an architectural style but then evolved into a form of playful yet exquisitely detailed painting. It evokes levity and frivolity with its cotton candy-like colors and youthful depictions of nature.

Concept Note: Drawing inspiration from the opulence and grace of the Rococo era, the Jubilard poster encapsulates the timeless luxury and charm of 18th-century aesthetics. The Rococo theme, known for its ornate details, pastel hues, and whimsical elegance, sets the stage for this exquisite ice cream brand. The soft pastel color palette, dominated by delicate shades of pink, blue, and cream, mirrors the romantic undertones of Rococo art. The swirling embellishments and intricate gold accents seen in the typography and decorative flourishes evoke the grandeur of aristocratic salons, where indulgence and beauty reigned supreme. The central imagery, featuring two ornate jars labeled Azure Royale and Pompadour Rose, celebrates the fusion of taste and art, evoking a sense of decadence and sophistication reminiscent of Marie Antoinette’s lavish lifestyle. The tagline, "Splendeur Gelée", or "Frozen Splendor," further amplifies the theme, suggesting that each scoop of Jubilard ice cream is not just a treat but an experience—a journey into a world of luxury, refinement, and sensory delight. This poster seamlessly bridges the past and the present, honoring the Rococo era's aesthetic while presenting Jubilard as a modern embodiment of artistry, indulgence, and sophistication.
